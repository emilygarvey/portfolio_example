About Me

“The core element of Google's mission is universal access and we drive our conviction and vision from that mission. And it's that everyone in the world should have affordable and abundant access to the full and open Internet.”

  • I grew up in New York City.
  • I attended Princeton University for college.
  • I earned my PhD in social psychology and quantitative analysis at the University of Southern California.
  • I have patented more than 200 of my ideas.
  • I have spent my career working at AT&T and Google.
  • I have earned many awards including for "Outstanding Technical Contribution – Industry” at the Black Engineer of the Year Awards (BEYA) in 2014.
  • I am on the board of the Holocaust and Human Rights Educational Center in New Jersey.
  • I have three children.
